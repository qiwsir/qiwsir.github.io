什么是学习编程的正确方法

2021-09-06

The high demand for programmers, developers, and data scientists attracted many people to join the field of IT. But, most often, newcomers try to take the fast track through the learning process to land a job faster.

对程序员、开发人员和数据科学家的高度需求吸引了许多人加入IT领域。但是，最常见的情况是，初学者试图在学习过程中走捷径，以便更快地找到工作。

Rushing the learning process is not always a good thing; in fact, sometimes, people may tend to skip over some important aspects of the field in their attempt to shorten their learning period or because they find them intimidating.

匆忙的学习过程不一定是好事；事实上，为了缩短学习过程，或者出于畏惧心理，人们可能跳过该领域的一些重要方面。

“The biggest mistake I see new programmers make is focusing on learning syntax instead of learning how to solve problems.” — V. Anton Spraul

“我发现：新手程序员犯的最大错误是专注于学习语法，而不是学习如何解决问题。” —— 安东·斯普拉尔

Regardless of your target career — software developer, web developer, or data scientist — all IT-based career share one thing in common, that is, programming knowledge.

无论你的目标职业是软件开发人员、web开发人员还是数据科学家，所有基于IT的职业都有一个共同点，那就是编程知识。

In this article, I will walk you through the 5 steps — I believe — the correct approach to learning to program. Before we start, I want to clarify I won’t be talking about how to program in a specific language. Rather, I will talk about developing the intuition and the thought process of solving a programming question.

在本文中，我将引导你完成5个步骤。我相信这是学习编程的正确方法。在开始之前，我想澄清一下，我不会谈论如何用特定的语言编程。相反，我将讨论如何培养解决编程问题的直觉和思维过程。

Being a programmer is more than just sitting in front of a computer clicking rapidly on a bunch of keys. When a programmer is given a programming question, sitting done and writing code is probably the last step.

作为一名程序员，你要做的可不仅仅是坐在电脑前快速点击很多的键。当程序员遇到一个编程问题时，坐下来写代码可能是最后一步。

Learn to solve problems logically first

首先学会有逻辑地解决问题

The first step is — and I know this is “old-fashioned” — solving the problem logically using a pen and a paper. Whenever I teach programming to a complete beginner, I start by explaining the logic behind solving programming problems and will use the pen and paper to solve many problems before we actually get into coding.

第一步是用纸和笔合乎逻辑地解决问题，我知道这种做法是“老式的”。每当我教一个零基础的初学者学习编程时，我都会在真正开始编码之前，先解释解决编程问题背后的逻辑，我会用笔和纸来解决许多问题。

To develop your programming intuition, you need to practice:

为了培养你的编程直觉，你需要以下练习:

  1. Learn to read problems carefully. Read a problem to find the details of exactly what you need to do, what your inputs are, and what your expected output is supposed to be.

    1.学会仔细阅读问题。阅读问题的目的在于找出具体细节，包括你需要做什么，你的输入是什么，以及你期望的输出是什么。

  2. Solve the problem without thinking of code first. I put this step first because if you already know how to code, you will tend to jump right into that. Stop! Solve the problem first using common words.

    2.在不考虑代码的情况下解决问题。 我把这一步放在第一位，因为如果你已经知道如何编码，你往往会直接开始写代码。 不要这样做! 首先用常用词解决问题。

  3. Test your solution on different input examples. Once you come up with a solution, try different case scenarios, and see if your solution holds up to them all.

3.在不同的输入示例上测试你的解决方案。一旦你想出了一个解决方案，就用它来尝试不同情形的案例，看看你的解决方案是否能满足所有的情形。

  1. Practice solving problems every day. Solving new problems every day will help you develop a programming intuition and practice your problem-solving skills. The daily coding problem is a good resource for that.

4.每天都练习解决问题。每天解决新问题将帮助你培养编程直觉，并锻炼你在解决问题方面的技能。每日编码问题是一个很好的资源。

This step is quite crucial, yet many seem to skip altogether. Learning the correct thought process and how to address programming problems will help reduce the time you spend solving problems in the future. It will also help you learn multiple programming languages faster and more efficiently. A great book about the process of problem-solving is Think Like a Programmer.

这个第一步非常关键，但许多人似乎完全忽略了这一步。学习正确的思维过程以及学习如何解决编程问题，这将有助于减少你在将来解决问题时所花费的时间。它还将帮助你更快、更高效地学习多种编程语言。《像程序员一样思考》是关于问题解决过程的一本好书。

Study algorithms and data structures

学习算法和数据结构

Many beginners and some experienced programmers avoid learning data structures and algorithms because they believe it’s complicated and not useful in real-life applications.

许多初学者和一些有经验的程序员避免学习数据结构和算法，因为他们认为数据结构和算法很复杂，在实际应用中没有用处。

Although you could — arguably — go somewhat far in your career without a solid knowledge of algorithms and data structures, having a good hold of these concepts will strengthen your knowledge base and allow you to be a better programmer.

可以说，尽管在没有扎实的算法和数据结构知识的情况下，你也可以在职业生涯中取得一定的成功。但掌握好这些概念将加强你的知识基础，让你成为一名更优秀的程序员。

Algorithms are not a computer-only concept. An algorithm is a set of steps that, when followed, solve a specific problem. If you really thought about it, we develop and use algorithms daily! When I wake up in the morning, I always make some coffee. I get the coffee beans, grind them, fill my coffee machine with fresh water, and brew my coffee.

算法的概念不仅仅适用于计算机。算法是一系列步骤，当遵循这些步骤时，可以解决特定的问题。如果你用心想一想，我们每天都在开发和使用算法！早上醒来时，我总是煮一些咖啡。我拿到咖啡豆，研磨它们，在我的咖啡机里装满淡水，然后煮咖啡。

I just followed a specific set of steps to solve a specific problem : my caffeine addiction !. When you learn how to solve a problem logically and develop intuition, you’re basically learning how to develop and apply algorithms.

我只是按照一套特定的步骤来解决一个特定的问题：我对咖啡因上瘾！当你学习如何有逻辑地解决问题并培养直觉时，你基本上是在学习如何开发和应用算法。

On the other hand, data structures are how your inputs and outputs are going to be presented. Back to my coffee algorithm, the coffee’s initial state will affect how I will prepare it. For example, if I have whole coffee beans, I will need to roast them and ground them first before I can brew my coffee. However, if the beans are already ground, I can jump straight to brewing them. Think of whole coffee beans and ground beans are the possible data structures for my coffee. Hence, different forms of data — or coffee — require different types of processing.

另一方面，数据结构是输入和输出的呈现方式。回到我的咖啡算法，咖啡的初始状态将影响我煮咖啡的方式。例如，如果我有整粒咖啡豆，我需要先把它们烤熟并磨碎，然后才能煮咖啡。然而，如果豆子已经磨碎了，我可以直接用它煮咖啡。对于煮咖啡这件事情来说，整个的咖啡豆和磨碎的咖啡豆是可能存在的数据结构。因此，不同形式的数据（或咖啡）需要不同的处理方式。

There are many resources out there that you can use to build a fundamental knowledge of algorithms and data structures. My favorite two are The Imposter Handbook and this Coursera course.

有很多资源可以用来构建算法和数据结构的基础知识。我最喜欢的两种资源是The Imposter Handbook ](https://bigmachine.io/products/the-imposters-handbook/)和[这个](https://www.coursera.org/specializations/data-structures-algorithms)慕课平台的课程。

Read about complexity theory.

阅读复杂性理论

Complexity theory is one of the vital aspects that everyone writes code for a living — regardless of what the code does — should be very familiar with.

对于以编写代码为生的每个人来说，复杂理论是非常重要、也应该非常熟悉的一个方面，不管这些代码的作用是什么。

Complexity theory describes how well an algorithm performs with respect to the size of the input and how it is implemented. In Layman’s term, it describes how good your algorithm is. Computers, although capable of performing impressive tasks, have their limitations. If you develop an algorithm that is so complex it current computers can’t run it, it might not be beneficial.

复杂性理论描述了算法在输入大小和实现方式方面的性能。用外行的话说，它描述了你的算法有多好。计算机虽然能够执行高难度的任务，但也有其局限性。如果你开发了一个非常复杂的算法，以至于现在的计算机无法运行它，那么它可能是没有好处的。

When you’re working on real-life applications, trying to tackle many problems, you always need to keep in mind your answers’ complexity. For example, if I gave you a book and asked you to open page number 5, you can simply go through pages 1~5. Because 5 is pretty near the beginning, passing through the p[ages before didn’t take much time. But say I wanted you to open up page 489. This will probably take you a little bit more time to do.

当你致力于现实生活中的应用程序，试图解决许多问题时，你总是需要记住答案的复杂性。例如，如果我给你一本书，让你打开第5页，你可以简单地翻阅第1~5页。因为第5页离开始很近，翻阅前几页并不需要很多时间。但是如果我想让你打开第489页，你可能需要多花费一点时间。

That’s what complexity theory describes. Although the problem is the same, different solutions can be prosed, not all of them will have the same effectiveness. Choosing the best solution is a skill that every developer needs to work on, and the base of that is complexity theory.

这就是复杂性理论所描述的特征。虽然问题是相同的，但可以提出不同的解决方案，但并非所有的解决方案都具有相同的效果。选择最佳解决方案是每个开发人员都需要掌握的技能，而复杂性理论就是掌握这一技能的基础。

The imposter handbook has a chapter on complexity theory that is very simple to follow. MIT and Standford also offer free courseware to learn about complexity theory if you want more in-depth information.

在The Imposter Handbook 这本书中有一章是关于复杂性理论的，很容易理解。如果你想要深入了解复杂性理论的相关信息，还可以利用[麻省理工学院和斯坦福大学提供的免费课件来学习。

Start with pseudo-code

从伪代码开始

Wait! It’s not the time yet to learn a specific programming language, but you’re almost there.

等一等！现在还不是学习一种特定的编程语言的时候，但是你已经快要做好准备了。

You have solved many problems, developed your intuition, and know the basic algorithms and data structures used. You want to start writing code and build cool projects.

你已经解决了许多问题，培养了你的直觉，并且了解了所使用的基本算法和数据结构。你希望开始编写代码并做一些很酷的项目。

But,

但是，

Before you do that, my advice is to implement your algorithms and problem-solving skills using pseudo-code first. As we just discussed, an algorithm is a set of steps used to solve a specific problem. Pseudocode is defined as “a plain language description of the steps in any algorithm.” This means pseudocode is used to describe an algorithm’s steps as a form between plain English and programming languages.

在此之前，我建议首先使用伪代码来实现你的算法和练就解决问题的技能。正如我们刚才讨论的，算法是用于解决特定问题的一套步骤。伪代码被定义为“任何算法中步骤的普通语言描述”。这意味着：我们用伪代码将算法的步骤描述为介于普通英语和编程语言之间的一种形式。

Often, pseudo-code is used for human reading rather than machine reading. It’s a way of explaining your thought process to other people and not to the computer.

通常，伪代码用于人工读取而不是机器读取。这是一种向他人而不是向计算机解释你的思维过程的方式。

Let me put it this way; if your algorithm doesn’t make sense to other programmers, it is even way complicated or inaccurate.

这样说吧：如果你的算法对其他程序员来说没有意义，那么它甚至会非常复杂或不准确。

[California Polytechnic State University ](http://California Polytechnic State University)has written a good guide on the standards of pseudo-code. Moreover, this DEV article explains how to write pseudo-code and when to use it.

加州理工州立大学编写了一本关于伪代码标准的优秀指南。此外，这一面向开发人员的文章解释了如何编写伪代码以及何时使用它。

Apply your knowledge to a specific syntax

将你的知识应用于特定语法

Finally, you are ready to start coding.

最后，你为开始编码做好了准备。

Once you’ve built a solid foundation of problem-solving, algorithms, and complexity theory, you can sit down and start choosing a programming language to start learning. According to Wikipedia, there are more than 700 programming languages out there. Not all of them are well-known or used today.

一旦你建立了解决问题、算法和复杂性理论的坚实基础，你就可以坐下来，开始选择并学习某一编程语言。根据维基百科，有700多种编程语言。现在，并非所有这些语言都是众所周知的、或仍在使用的。

At this point, you might ask yourself which programming language to start with?

此时，你可能会问自己：从哪种编程语言开始学起？

The good news is, all programming languages are fundamentally the same. This means if you have a well-developed programming intuition, you can apply it to any programming language. You just have to learn its syntax.

好消息是，所有编程语言基本相同。这意味着，如果你有良好的编程直觉，你可以将这种直觉应用于任何编程语言。你只需要学习它的语法。

I want you to think of the different programming languages as different ways to say the same thing. For example, if I want to say that there is a nice garden near my house, I can probably say:

我想让你们把不同的编程语言看作是表达同一事物的不同方式。例如，如果我想说我家附近有一个漂亮的花园，我可能会说：

  • There is a beautiful garden near my house.
  • -我家附近有一个美丽的花园。
  • I absolutely love the garden near my house.
  • -我非常喜欢我家附近的花园。
  • I enjoy the beauty of the garden near my house.
  • -我喜欢我家附近花园的美景。
  • There is a garden near my house. It is beautiful.
  • -我家附近有一个花园。它很美丽。

All programming languages have the same vocabulary, variables, loops, data types, conditional statements, functions, class, etc. They just have slightly different ways of writing them.

所有编程语言都有相同的词汇表、变量、循环、数据类型、条件语句、函数、类等。它们的编写方式略有不同。

So, no matter which programming language you start with, you can learn another syntax in no time, provided your foundation is solid. That being said, [based on your target career](https://hackr.io/blog/best-programming-languages-to-learn-2020-jobs-future#:~:text=Python undoubtedly tops the list,to develop scalable web applications.), some programming languages may be better to learn than others.

所以，不管你使用哪种编程语言，只要你的基础是可靠的，你很快就能学会另一种语法。话虽如此，基于你的目标职业，有些编程语言可能比其他语言更值得学习。

Final thoughts

最后的想法

Regardless of your target career path, if you want to build an IT career, you will need to learn and be good at programming. Although there are many paths you can follow to learn how to program, the correct path — in my opinion — is:

不管你的目标职业道路是什么，如果你想要建立一个IT职业，你都需要学习并擅长编程。 虽然有很多路径你可以遵循学习如何编程，在我看来，正确的路径是:

  1. Develop a good programming intuition (work on problem-solving skills).

    培养良好的编程直觉(解决问题的技能)。

  2. Study algorithms and data structures.

学习算法和数据结构。

  1. Learn at least the basics of complexity theory.

至少学习复杂性理论的基础知识。

  1. Implement your solutions using pseudo-code first.

首先使用伪代码实现解决方案。

  1. Learn the syntax of any programming language.

学习任何编程语言的语法。

If you follow the first 4 steps, the language you choose in the 5th step will not matter much. Think of the first 4 steps as the pillars of your knowledge; the stronger the pillars, the higher you can go with learning.

如果你遵循前四步，你在第五步选择的语言就不会有太大的影响。 把前四步看作你的知识支柱; 支柱越强大，可以取得的造诣也就越高。
